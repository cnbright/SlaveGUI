TFT LCD Integrated Power Module for Gaming Notebook  
General Description 
The RT6755 is an I2C interface programmable power 
management IC. The IC  includes one synchronous 
boost converters for PAVDD, one synchronous NAVDD 
buck-boost, one SIBO (Single Inductor Bipolar Output) 
converter for VGH and VGL, one high performance 
VCOM with 8- bit Calibrator, two GMA1/2 and one 
RESET voltage detector with available in a 
UQFN-28TL 3.5x3.5 (FC) package, this device is 
suitable for gaming notebook TFT-LCD panel.  
The IC can operate from 2.5V to 5V input voltages. 
High switching frequency operation allows a compact 
PCB circuit area with small inductors and capacit ors. 
Current-limit functions are provided for all internal - 
switch converters, and output -fault shutdown protects 
all converters against output -fault conditions. 
Programmable soft-start functions for all output voltage 
to limit input inrush current during startup.

Features 
 2.5V to 5V Input Supply Range 
 I2C Interface

 Output Current Capability up to 400mA 
 Current Limitation Protection 
 NAVDD Synchronous Inverting Converter 
 Programmable Output Voltage −4V to −7V 
 Output Current Capability up to 400mA 
 Current Limitation Protection 
 VGH / VGL Synchronous SIBO Converter 
 VGH Programmable Output Range from 6V to 
30V 
 VGL Programmable Output Range −6V to −18V 
 VGH and VGL Output Current Capability up to 
20mA 
 VCORE and VIO Synchronous Buck Converter 
 VCORE Programmable Output Range from 0.8V 
to 2V 
 VIO Programmable Output Range from 1V to 
2.5V 
 Low Dropout Voltage Linear Regulator 
 VCOM 8bits Programmable Output Voltage 
 GMA1/2 Programmable Gamma Output Voltage 
 Programmable Voltage Detector 
 Built in UVLO, UVP, OVP , SCP and OTP 
protection 
 RoHS Compliant and Halogen Free

Applications Information 
The RT6755 is an integrated solution for TFT LCD 
panel, including PMIC and memory system. The 
RT6755 application mechanism is introduced in later 
sections. The RT6755 ’s 7 -bits slave address is 
0100_011. 
PMIC - Power management system provides a 
sync-boost converters  for PAVDD , one synchronous 
inverting converter for NAVDD, a low dropout LDO, two 
synchronous Buck converters, a SIBO (Single Inductor 
Bipolar Output) converter for VGH and VGL, one digital 
VCOM operational amplifier with 7 bits calibrator, a 
high performance operation amplifier, 2- CH gamma, 
and a voltage detector. All channels of converters are 
programmable, such as output voltage, power on 
sequence, switching frequency of the converters, and 
to disable each output channel selection. 
Channel Setting 
The RT6755 can enable or disable each output by I2C 
interface. Users can control these outputs of PAVDD, 
NAVDD, VGH, VGL, VCORE, VIO, LDO, VCOM, 
GMA1, GMA2, and RESET, through programing the 
02h and 03h register. The PAVDD, NAVDD, VGH, VGL, 
VCOM, NAVDD, VCORE, V IO, LDO, GMA1 , GMA2 
and RESET can be disable individually. Please refer  
the register map for details. 
Under Voltage Protection (UVP) 
The RT6755 equip a fault conditions to shut down IC. 
Once t he output voltage is below the 80% output 
voltage, the internal timer starts counting and the f ault 
condition continued about 60ms, the IC will shut down. 
The fault protection would be keep until VIN falls below 
UVLO. The protection start detecting at the soft -start 
finish of the outputs channel, the voltage of outputs 
should be larger than the UVP level afte r soft -start 
finish and within 6 0ms. The protection behavior is 
summarized in Table 2. 
Short Circuit Protection (SCP) 
The RT6755 equips SCP  protection to shut down IC  
from damage.  
For PAVDD and VGH, once output voltage falls below 
1.3V, the converter  will stop switching immediately . If 
the fault condition does not release within 60ms after 
SCP occurred, all the other channels will be shut down 
and IC will latch.  
For NAVDD, VIO, Vcore  and VGL, once t he output 
voltage is below 20% of target  voltage, the 
corresponding converter will stop switching 
immediately. If the fault condition does not release 
within 60ms after SCP occurred, all the other channels 
will be shut down and latch.  
The protection is released when UVLO is cycled.  
Please refer to the table 2 for the details. 
Over Temperature Protection (OTP) 
The RT 6755 equips an over temperature protection 
(OTP) to prevent the excessive power dissipation from 
overheating. The OTP will shut down switching 
operation while junction temperature exceeds 
approximately 1 60°C. All of output channel starting 
work while junction temperature is cooled by 
approximately 2 5°C. Prevent the maximum junction 
temperature over around 15 0 ℃  and maintain 
continuous operation. Please refer to the table 2 for the 
details. 
PAVDD Synchronous Boost Converter 
The PAVDD synchronous Boost converter is high 
efficiency PWM architecture with progr ammable 
switching frequency. It performs fast transient 
responses to meet the requirement of source driver 
supplies for TFT -LCD display.  The output voltage is 
controlled by a 6- bits register with 60  steps. The error 
amplifier varies the COMP voltage by sensing the 
PAVDD pin to regulate the output voltage. 
PAVDD Switching Frequency Setting 
The switching frequency is set by the I 2C interface, the 
corresponding register is at 0x10h [2:0].  There are 5 
steps in frequency setting for boost converter, the 
options are 600kHz, 715kHz, 800kHz, 9 00kHz, 
1000kHz and 12 00kHz. The switching frequency 
default value is 1200kHz (0x05h). Please refer to the 
register map for details.

for the positive (VGH) output voltage and an inverter 
mode for the negative (VGL) output voltage. The output 
voltage, switching frequency, soft -start time and delay 
time setting all can be controlled by the I 2C interface. 
For flexibility in applications, the SIBO converter input, 
VINP, can be connected to either input voltage from 
typical VIN or PAVDD output. 
VGH Output Voltage Setting 
The VGH output voltages are set by the I 2C interface. 
Users can write the 01h[ 7] and 07h[4:0] register to set 
VGH output voltage. The resolution of output voltage 
can be adjusted by programing register 01h[7]. It has a 
5-bit register for output voltage adjustment, the setting 
range is from 10V to 30V if  01h[7]=0x00h, and each 
voltage step is 1V. The default output voltage of VGH is 
18V (0x08h). On the contrary, the setting range is from 
6V to 10V at 01h[7]=0x01h, and each voltage step is 
200mV. Please refer to the register map for details. 
VGL Output Voltage Setting 
The VGL output voltages are set by the I 2C interface. 
Users can write 08h[5:0] register to set VGL output 
voltage. It has a 6- bit register for output voltage 
adjustment, the setting range is from -5.4V to -18V. The 
default output voltage of VGL is -14V (0x2Bh). Please 
refer to the register map for details. 
VGH/VGL Switching Frequency Setting 
The switching frequency of SIBO converter is set by I2C 
interface. It has a 3- bits register with 6 steps. The 
switching frequency range is from 600kHz to 1200 kHz. 
The switching frequency default value is 1200kHz 
(0x05h). Please refer to the register map for details. 
VGH/VGL Delay Time Setting 
The VGH/VGL SIBO converter has integrated a delay 
function. The delay time is adjustable by the I 2C 
interface. Both VGH/VGL have a 4-bits register with 16 
steps. The delay tim e setting range is from 0ms to 
75ms, and each step change is 5ms. The VG L delay 
time default value is 15ms  (16h[3:0]=0x03h), the VGH 
delay time default value is 25ms (15h[3:0]=0x05h). The 
delay time is defined from the VIN rises over the UVLO 
to VGL (VGH) output voltage starts falling (rising). For 
most application, it’s recommended that set VGH delay 
time larg er than VGL delay  time plus VGL soft start 
time. Please refer to the Timing Diagram, and register 
map for details.

VGH/VGL Soft-start Time Setting 
The VGH/VGL SIBO converter has an integrated 
soft-start function to reduce the input inrush current 
when power on. It has a 2- bits register with 4 steps. 
The soft-start time setting range is from 4ms to 12ms. 
The VG L soft -start time default value is 4 ms 
(16h[5:4]=0x00h). The VGH soft -start time default  
value is 4ms (15h[5:4]=0x00h). The soft - start time is 
defined from the VGL (VGH) output voltage start falling 
(Rising) to VGL (VGH) output voltage ready. Please 
refer to the Timing Diagram, and register map for 
details. 
LDO Output voltage Setting 
The LDO linear regulator can supply up to 100mA 
current with an input voltage of 3.3V. It uses an internal 
P-MOSFET as the pass device. The LDO output 
voltage setting can be achi eved by setting I2C register 
(0Bh[3:0]) from 1.8V to 3V. It has 4bit with 13  steps. 
The LDO default value is 2.5V(0x07h). 
LDO Delay time Setting 
The LDO linear regulator has integrated a delay 
function. The delay time is adjustable by the I2C 
interface. It has a 4-bits register with 10 steps, user can 
write the 0Bh[7:4] register to set LDO delay time. The 
delay time setting range is from 0ms to 45ms, and each 
step change is 5ms. The delay time default value is 
10ms (0x02h). The delay time is defined from  the VIN 
rises over the UVLO to LDO output voltage starts rising. 
Please refer to the Timing Diagram, and register map 
for details. 
Programmable VCOM 
The RT6755 provides the ability to reduce the flicker of 
an LCD Panel by adjusting the VCOM voltage during 
production test and alignment. The output voltage is 
adjusted by the I2C interface. There are two registers to 
adjust the VCOM voltage, one is VCOM for coarse tune, 
and another one is D-VCOM with independent slave ID 
0x9Eh for fine tune. It is suggested to connect a 
resistor 10 ohm between output pin and output 
capacitor for better stability. 
In general application, VCOM should not be set 0V. If 
user don’t want to use this channel, please disable it. 
VCOM Power-On Delay Time Setting 
The VCOM is integrated power-on sequence control. 
The delay time is adjustable by I 2C interface, there are 
5 bits within register 0Dh[7:3]. The delay tim e setting 
range is from 0ms to 155ms, and each step is 5ms. The 
default value is 25ms (0x05h ). The delay time is 
defined from  the VIN rises over the UVLO to VCOM 
output voltage starts falling (rising).  Please refer the 
Figure 5, and register map for detail. 
VCOM Voltage Setting 
The VCOM voltage is adjusted by the register 
0x0Ch[7:0], it is provided 8bits resolution and 182 steps 
for user setting.  The setting range is from - 2.56V to 
1.06V, each step is 20mV. The default value is - 1V 
(0x4Eh). Please refer the register map for detail. 
D-VCOM Voltage Setting 
The D-VCOM is used to fine tune VCOM voltage and is 
programmable by I 2C interface as well , but has  
independent slave ID 0x9E that is different than VCOM. 
User can adjust it with “ D-VCOM I2C Write Timing 
Sequence” section in I2C the following. The D -VCOM 
provides 128 steps within 7 bits resolution. The default 
value is equal to VCOM if set D -VCOM as 0x40.  
Adjusting DVCOM upward from code 0x40 (center) 
means increasing VCOM finely, each step is 10mV 
upward. On the contray, adjusting DVCOM downward 
from code 0x40 (center) means decreasing VCOM 
finely, each step is 10mV downward. The setting range 
is from (VCOM + 0.63V) to (VCOM – 0.64V). Please 
refer the register map for detail. 
GMA1/GMA2  
The RT6755 provides 2- CH gamma correction under 
6-bits resolution. The adjustment of the output is 
provided by the I 2C interface. Users can write the 0Eh 
(0Fh) register to set GMA1 (GMA2) output voltage. The 
GMA1 setting range is from PAVDD to PAVDD-1.26V. 
The GMA2 setting range is from NAVDD+1.26V to 
NAVDD.

RESET Voltage Detector 
The voltage detector monitors the VIN voltage to 
generate a RESET signal from RESET pin while VIN is 
lower than the detecting level and not latched. Both 
detecting level and power -on delay time could be set 
by I2C interface. The detecting level could b e adjusted 
by the register (0x0D h [2:0]), it provided 6 options 
within 3 bits . The delay tim e could be set by register 
(0x1B[3:0]), the setting range is from 0ms to 75ms, the 
each step is about 15ms. The delay time start from that 
VIN over UVLO threshold, the stop point is that RESET 
signal goes to high. 
In addition, the voltage d etector can be turned off via 
I2C interface 1Bh[5:4].  There are four options can be 
selected: always high, or after RESET function is 
triggered, keep low in 20ms, 40ms, 60ms , then return 
to high level . Please refer to the Timing Diagram, and 
register map for details. 
 
Discharge Function 
The PAVDD, NAVDD, VGH, VGL , VIO, Vcore  and 
VCOM outputs voltage is integrated a discharge 
function. The each output v oltage discharged from 
100% to 20% rapidly , preventing phenomena such as 
residual image on the display at power -off. The 
discharge function also provided an option for user to 
enable or disable, the o ption can set by the register 
04h[8:0] individually for each channel. 
Control Register (FFH) 
The RT6755 provides a register for user choosing that 
write/read data into MTP or register. User can set the 
MSB of the register FFH goes to high, it means the 
data is written into MTP. But writing data into register 
don’t need to set the register FFH. In addition, reading 
data from MTP need to set the LSB of register FFH to 
high. On the contrary, reading data from register need 
to set LSB to low. Please refer the “I 2C Write/Read 
Timing Sequence” for detail.

Thermal Considerations 
The junction temperature should never exceed the 
absolute maximum junction temperature T J(MAX), listed 
under Absolute Maximum Ratings, to avoid permanent 
damage to the device. The maximum allowable power 
dissipation depends on the thermal resistance of the IC 
package, the PCB layout, the rate of surrounding 
airflow, and the difference between the junction and 
ambient temperatures. The maxim um power 
dissipation can be calculated using the following 
formula : 
PD(MAX) = (TJ(MAX) − TA) / θJA 
where T J(MAX) is the maximum junction temperature, 
TA is the ambient temperature, and θJA is the 
junction-to-ambient thermal resistance. 
For continuous operation, the maximum operating 
junction temperature indicated under Recommended 
Operating Conditions is 125°C. The junction-to- 
ambient thermal resistance, θJA, is highly package 
dependent. For a UQFN -28TL 3.5x3.5 package, the 
thermal resistance, θ JA, is  31.2°C/W on a standard 
JEDEC 51 -7 high effective- thermal-conductivity 
four-layer test board. The maximum power dissipation 
at TA = 25°C can be calculated as below :  
PD(MAX) = (125° C − 25°C) / (31.2° C/W) = 3. 2W for a 
UQFN-28TL 3.5x3.5 package. 
The maximum  power dissipation depends on the 
operating ambient temperature for the fixed T J(MAX) 
and the thermal resistance, θJA. The derating curves in 
Figure 2 allows the designer to see the effect of rising 
ambient temperature on the maximum power 
dissipation.

Layout Consideration 
For the best performance of the RT6755. The following 
descriptions are the guidelines for better PCB layout :  
 The power components such as inductor(L1, L2, L3, 
L4, L5), input cap(C 10, C26, C27, C40) and output 
cap(C4, C5, C6, C7, C16, C17, C20, C21, C23, C24, 
C29, C30, C31, C32, C44, C45, C46, C47) must be 
placed as close as possible to reduce power loop. 
The PCB trace between power components must be 
as short and wide as possible. 
 Minimize the size of the LX , LXI, LXB1, LXB2, LXP, 
LXN node and keep it wide and short. Keep the LX, 
LXI, LXB1, LXB2, LXP  and LXN node away from 
those sensing pins (COMP, VCOM, NEG ) and 
analog ground. 
 The power ground (PGNDB, PGND) consists of input 
and output capacitor grounds. 
 The compensation circuit (R 7, C13) should be kept 
away from the power loops and should be shielded 
with a ground trace to prevent any noise coupling. 
Place the compensation components as close as 
possible to COMP pin. 
 The exposed pad of the chip should be connected to 
a ground plane for thermal consideration.
